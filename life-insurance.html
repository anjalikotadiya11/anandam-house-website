What is Life Insurance?

Life Insurance is a contract between an insurance policyholder and an insurance company, where the insurer promises to pay a designated beneficiary a sum of money upon the death of the insured person. It provides financial protection to your family and loved ones in case of your untimely demise.

Types of Life Insurance

Term Life Insurance

Pure protection policy that provides coverage for a specific period. Affordable premiums with high coverage amounts.

  • Fixed premium for the term
  • No cash value
  • Ideal for young families

Whole Life Insurance

Permanent coverage that provides protection for your entire life with guaranteed cash value accumulation.

  • Lifetime coverage
  • Cash value component
  • Guaranteed death benefit

Endowment Plans

Combines protection with savings, paying out either on death or at the end of the policy term.

  • Maturity benefit
  • Death benefit
  • Regular premium payment

ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plans)

Investment-cum-insurance products that offer market-linked returns along with life coverage.

  • Market-linked returns
  • Flexible premium payment
  • Multiple fund options

Money Back Plans

Provides periodic survival benefits during the policy term along with life coverage.

  • Survival benefits
  • Death benefit
  • Maturity benefit

Child Plans

Specially designed to secure your child's future education and marriage expenses.

  • Education funding
  • Marriage expenses
  • Waiver of premium benefit
